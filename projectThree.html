A SCHOOL MANAGEMENT SYSTEM APP FOR PUBLIC SCHOOLS

My role: UI/UX designer | Duration: 6 months


OVERVIEW

This project was done at Teckplus in Nigeria. Teckplus is a young and growing tech company in Lagos that is committed to bringing about transformation through innovations and inventions.

The services they offer include web development, mobile app development, cyber security, IOT and machine learning. The project is a comprehensive school management desktop application which is responsive and suitable for different screen sizes.


Identifying the problems

Initially, the first version of the app was designed to be used internationally. However, after the deployment in a public school in Lagos, we noticed that the app was too comprehensive. Therefore, we started another version with the aim of simplifying it and removing unnecessary features so that users can have better experiences.

Prior to the development of the second version, we updated our design system to a modern version. So we seized the opportunity to design a new version of the app that is in compliance with our new design system.







Users and audience

The users of the app include the public school officials in Lagos (principals, vice-principals, teachers, district officials, state officials) and the students. Unlike the first version of the app which was designed to be used worldwide, the second version was targeted at the public schools in Lagos.

These schools run a system of management which is different from other schools in other states in Nigeria in which a district controls all the activities of the schools under it. The district dictate the curriculum and also influence other vital part of the admission process.




My roles and responsibilities

As at the time the first version of the app was designed, I was not part of the company's design team. I got employed when the designing of the second version of the app began. I had the opportunity of working with two other product designers, two front-end developers and two back-end developers.

I was responsible for designing different part of the school portal, state portal and the district portal. We had series of meetings with the frontend and backbend team during which we collaborated on the the designs and how it would be implemented.



Scope and constraints

The development of the second version of the app was smooth and easy. This is because the team had learnt different lessons from the deployment of the first version. The biggest problem we encountered in the development of the second version came during the deployment process. Most of the schools did not have the important details needed for the deployment ready. They had to search through files and make different calls to get them which wasted the time of our team and slowed down the deployment process.

Also, some of the schools tried to claim ownership of the app by requesting that the important parts of the app only bear their logos. We dealt with this problem by clearly defining the terms and agreement guiding the usage of the app and also modified certain aspect of the app to accommodate the design needs of the schools.



The process

We started the design process by doing a qualitative research. We conducted interviews with the users at the districts to know their needs, understand their behaviors and attitudes. We created user personas and user story based on the information we gathered.

We used figma for both the wireframing and prototyping of this project. We created a design system to simplify the design process and ensure consistency throughout the project. As as team, we designed the desktop, tablet and mobile views for all the portals. We had several meetings with the frontend and backend teams where we collaborated on the design and exchanged ideas.










User testing

The design was tested by different group of users from the district and the schools. Most of the users were able to easily navigate the app with ease.



Outcome and lesson

  • This project taught me how to work in a team with other designers and developers. Most of the work I had done prior to this was done by me alone.

  • The project taught me how to be consistent in user interface and user experience design.

  • I learnt how to communicate my ideas better to other members of a team.
